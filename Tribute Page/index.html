Sebastian Vettel

The 4 time F1 world champion

The following are the journey, achievements,and some key moments in the racing journey of Sebastian Vettel.

Sebastian Vettel's journey in racing began in his childhood with go-karting, where his talent and determination quickly became evident. He honed his skills on the karting circuits of his native Germany, showing promise and securing multiple championship victories at a young age. His success in karting caught the attention of motorsport scouts, leading to his transition to single-seater racing. In the early stages of his career, Vettel competed in various junior formula series, including Formula BMW and Formula 3, showcasing his speed and racecraft. His impressive performances in these categories earned him recognition as a rising star in motorsport. In 2007, Vettel made his Formula 1 debut as a substitute driver for the BMW Sauber team, marking the beginning of his remarkable journey in the pinnacle of motorsport.

Early Career and Red Bull Racing:

  • 2007: Made his Formula 1 debut with BMW Sauber as a substitute driver.
  • 2008: Secured a full-time drive with Scuderia Toro Rosso and achieved his maiden victory at the Italian Grand Prix, becoming the youngest race winner at the time.
  • 2009: Joined Red Bull Racing and won his first race with the team at the Chinese Grand Prix.
  • 2010: Clinched his first Formula 1 World Championship title, becoming the youngest champion in Formula 1 history at the age of 23, after a dramatic season finale in Abu Dhabi.
  • 2011: Dominated the season, securing 11 race victories and his second consecutive World Championship title.
  • 2012: Fought off challenges from rivals to secure his third consecutive World Championship, winning five races and achieving consistent podium finishes.
  • 2013: Achieved his fourth consecutive World Championship title, winning 13 races out of 19 and setting multiple records, including nine consecutive race wins in a single season.

Move to Ferrari:

  • 2015: Joined Scuderia Ferrari, aiming to help the team return to championship contention.
  • 2017: Finished second in the drivers' championship behind Lewis Hamilton, scoring five race wins and multiple podium finishes.
  • 2018: Continued to challenge for wins and podiums with Ferrari, but faced tough competition from Mercedes and Red Bull Racing.
  • 2019: Experienced challenges with Ferrari, including team orders and strategic issues, but managed to secure two race wins in Singapore and Canada.

Aston Martin (formerly Racing Point):

  • 2021: Signed with Aston Martin (formerly Racing Point) for the 2021 season, bringing his experience to the rebranded team.
  • 2022: Continued to race for Aston Martin, aiming to help the team compete at the front of the grid in a highly competitive Formula 1 field.
  • By the end of end of 2022 F1 season Vettel retired from the sport.

Throughout his career, Sebastian Vettel has not only showcased remarkable talent behind the wheel but has also demonstrated leadership and resilience in challenging situations, cementing his status as one of the greatest drivers in Formula 1 history.
